Adventures in Fire Island

Last weekend, I took a quiet trip to Fire Island, and it felt like leaving the world behind. With no cars and only boardwalks and bicycles, everything moved at a softer pace. I walked along the beach early in the morning, the sand still cool beneath my feet, while gentle waves echoed across an almost empty shoreline. The air smelled of salt and pine, and for a while it felt like the ocean was speaking only to me.

As the day went on, I explored small cafés, colorful beach houses, and peaceful trails. By sunset, the sky turned pink and lavender over the water. Sitting there, watching the waves roll in, I felt a calm I hadn’t felt in a long time. Fire Island wasn’t just a place — it was a reminder to breathe.
